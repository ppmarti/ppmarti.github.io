Cluster algebras

A cluster algebra is a certain kind of commutative algebra contained in the field F of quotients of polynomials in n variables x1,x2,...,xn. The definition is combinatorial and (in the simplest case) relies on an initial choice of a directed graph. This graph is then modified via a so-called "mutation" process which determines generators for the algebra.

Cluster algebras first arose in the theory of the dual canonical basis of the quantized enveloping algebra, a central object in Lie theory. Although they have an elementary definition, they have good properties and links with many other fields, including the representation theory of quivers, finite dimensional algebras, Riemannian surfaces and combinatorics.

The cluster algebras of finite type have a particularly beautiful structure, with a close connection to root systems (finite subsets of R^n used to define groups generated by reflections) and their combinatorics. The main initial aim of the project is to describe the cluster algebras of finite type. There is scope for development in a variety of directions, such as the more combinatorial side of cluster algebras or the representation theoretic side.

The project is available for the modules MATH 3082, MATH 3083 and MATH 5082M. A student taking this project must have passed MATH 1022 Introductory Group Theory, MATH 2032 Rings, Polynomials and Fields and MATH 2080 Further Linear Algebra, and either have passed or be taking MATH 3071 Groups and Symmetry.

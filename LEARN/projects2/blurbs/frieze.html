Frieze Patterns of Integers

A frieze pattern is an infinitely long strip containing a repeating pattern. It is known that there are 7 types of symmetry amongst such patterns. Coxeter and Conway consider the notion of a frieze pattern of integers, in which integers are repeated instead of patterns. A frieze pattern of integers contains several rows of integers subject to certain relations, and Coxeter and Conway give a classification of frieze patterns in terms of triangulations of polygons.

The project will involve studying frieze patterns and their relationship with triangulations of polygons. There are also a number of related areas for possible study, including the geometric meaning of the entries of a frieze pattern and a determinantal relation, additive frieze patterns, algebraic study of frieze patterns, and the links with cluster algebras.

This project is primarily combinatorial with links with algebra.

Books

Initial reference:
J. H. Conway and H. S. M. Coxeter, Triangulated polygons and frieze patterns, The Mathematical Gazette, Vol. 57, Np. 400 (June 1973), pages 87-94.

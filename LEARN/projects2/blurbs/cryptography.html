Cryptography

Codes are as old, or even older than mathematics. But the recent past has seen great advances in encryption, particularly with so-called public key encryption methods. The most famous of these, the RSA method, is simple enough to be explainable to schoolchildren, but relies on Euler's extension of a result of Fermat's, and depends on the difficulty of factorizing into primes for its efficacy.

This project is only avalable within the level 4 module MATH5082.

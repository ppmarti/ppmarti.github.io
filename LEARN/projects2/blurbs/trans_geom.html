Transformation Geometry

Felix Klein viewed Geometry as the study of properties invariant under a group of transformations on a space. From this viewpoint two-dimensional Euclidean geometry is the study of isometries of the plane. Changing the group of transformations leads to other interesting and useful geometries: affine, projective, spherical, hyperbolic and Minkowskian (this last being the natural geometry of spacetime in Einstein's special relativity).

Prerequisites: Level 2 linear algebra (MATH 2200, MATH 2080, or equivalent), and MATH 1022 Introductory Group Theory.

Books

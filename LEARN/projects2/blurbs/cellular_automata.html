Cellular Automata

A cellular automaton consists of a regular grid of cells, each in one of a finite number of states. These evolve at times t=1,2,3,... according to specific rules. The most famous example is probably Conway's game of Life. For a good non-mathematical introduction to this, take a look at the Wikipedia article on the Internet. Such a project would involve working through some of the mathematical work of Wolfram and other authors, although there are no prerequisites beyond second year pure mathematics.

Books

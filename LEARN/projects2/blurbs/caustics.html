Caustics

Caustics occur throughout the natural world. You can see one on the surface of a coffee cup (you'll need a single light source and milk in your coffee); it is the curve of bright light. Usually the curve has cusp on it, that is a sharp point. The patterns of light you can see at the bottom of a swimming pool are also caustics.

This assignment focuses on the geometry of these interesting curves and in particular what happens when we move the mirrored surface that produces them.

Prerequisites: MATH2051 Geometry of Curves and Surfaces.

Books

Gibson C.G., Elementary Geometry of Differentiable Curves, in particular Chapter 12.
Bruce, J. W.; Giblin, P. J.; Gibson, C. G. On caustics of plane curves. Amer. Math. Monthly 88 (1981), no. 9, 651--667.
Bruce, J. W.; Giblin, P. J.; Gibson, C. G. Caustics through the looking glass. Math. Intelligencer 6 (1984), no. 1, 47--58.

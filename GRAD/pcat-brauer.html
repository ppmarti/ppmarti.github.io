Asides On Brauer Algebras

\section{Yang-Baxter} Recall that the Brauer and Temperley-Lieb categories are monoidal (i.e. tensor) subcategories of the partition category; and the Brauer algebras are the end-sets in the Brauer category. But besides its incarnation as a subalgebra of the partition algebra, the Brauer algebra has a role in invariant theory (indeed this is its original motivation [Brauer]). It follows from this aspect that it can be used to construct solutions to the Yang-Baxter equation [Zamolodchikov]. (One form for this is: \[ R_1(u) R_2(u+v) R_1(v) = R_2(v) R_1(u+v) R_2(u) \] - see later.) The Yang-Baxter equation is a condition for certain operators to commute, that is useful in computational statistical mechanics [Baxter,Yang]. However this in turn can be used to provide one approach to (certain questions in) Brauer algebra representation theory.

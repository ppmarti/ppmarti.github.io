Essential Reading

I tend to assume you know the material in these works, so they (or some equivalents) are essential reading.

  • R H Crowell and R H Fox, Introduction to Knot Theory, (Ginn, 1963).
      Here I don't care about KNOTS per se, but this is a handy place to learn about some basic topology and geometry...
    • category
    • fundamental groupoid
    • regular projection
    • ...
  • M Hamermesh, Group Theory, (Dover).
    • symmetric group
    • (alternative to Hamermesh) H Boerner, Representations of groups, (North-Holland).
  • J Kogut, An Introduction to Lattice Gauge Theory and Spin Systems (Rev Mod Phys, 1979).
  • E Lieb and Mattis, Mathematical Physics in One Dimension, (AP).
  • S Mac Lane, Categories for the working mathematician, (Springer).
  • E E Moise, Geometric Topology, (Springer).

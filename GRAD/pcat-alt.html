%\setcounter{section}{1} %\setcounter{subsection}{4} \subsection{Alternative constructions} The partition algebra started life as the transfer matrix algebra for Potts models ([M91]). The $Q$-state Potts model has a global $S_Q$ symmetry, so (unsurprisingly one finds that) the Potts representation of the partition algebra encodes invariant theory of the symmetric group. Correspondingly the $Q$-indeterminate version encodes $Q$-stable properties of invariant theory. In particular consider [Hamermesh] Sec.7-13 on Inner products (Kronecker products) and Clebsch-Gordan series for the symmetric group. We extend this in [MW98]. Invariant theory is frequently addressed via some form of `diagram calculus'. Weyl's diagrams for the Brauer algebra [Brauer37] are among the first examples; and partition diagrams are a natural extension. A nice complementary review, from the group theory perspective, of various forms of diagram calculus is in Cvitanovic .
